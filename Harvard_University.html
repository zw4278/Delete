Our climate priorities

Harvard has outlined an ambitious goal to be fossil-fuel neutral by 2026 and fossil-fuel free by 2050. Were focused on achieving this in three ways:

Climate research and innovation

Harvard students, faculty, researchers, and staff are working every day to uncover breakthrough solutions to our climate crisis.

Explore our climate research

Global and local sustainability

Across our local campus and global community, were working to become more energy efficient and sustainable.

Visit Green Harvard

Carbon-neutral investing

We are reducing our endowment portfolio’s carbon footprint, using our investment strategy to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2050.

Read President Bacow’s statement on climate change

Shifting to solar

Since 2003, Harvard has installed more than 2.08 megawatts of on-site solar on our campus roughly equivalent to 415 home installations. Were also partnering with our local utility to increase the amount of renewable energy in the regional electric grid and help Massachusetts meet its renewable energy target.
Learn about our sustainability goals

Innovating new models

Between 2017-2018, a team at the Harvard Graduate School of Design transformed a 1940s house into an office space that is net-zero, producing more energy than it consumes. Today, HouseZero is a living lab for data collection and analysis and a model for our future.
Learn about HouseZero

Greener buildings

In 2015, we broke the record to become the most LEED-certified higher education institution in the world. Today, our 136 certified sites are resulting in reduced carbon emissions, greater energy and water efficiency, and higher quality indoor experiences for our on-campus community.
Read the Harvard Gazette article

Raising food standards

In 2019, Harvard developed new sustainable food standards to provide healthier options on campus and to reduce the climate impact of our food programs. Food accounts for more than 25% of greenhouse gas emissions globally and Harvard is prioritizing choices that will lead to a healthier, more sustainable future.
Learn more about the sustainable food program

Greener commutes

In 2019, 85% of Harvard Cambridge/Allston commuters and 81% of Harvard Longwood commuters used sustainable transportation options to get to work. Of those who commuted in 2019, 38% used public transit, 24% walked, and 15% biked.
View the Harvard Gazette gallery
